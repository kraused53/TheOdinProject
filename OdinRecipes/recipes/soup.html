Dan's Chicken Soup

When I was in college, I worked in a kosher kitchen that provided Jewish students (and anyone else who came in) with a warm home-style Shabbat meal every week. I learned how to make many classic Jewish dishes: challah, brisket, stuffed cabbage, and, of course, matzoh ball soup. This recipe is based on the soup I helped make every Friday morning, although I no longer make it kosher (don't tell the Rabbi). This delicious soup can be used as a stock if you do not add the shredded chicken or finishing vegetables. It can also be made vegan (and thus pareve!) by simply leaving out the chicken.

Ingredients

  • 4 Carrots
  • 2 Parsnips
  • 2 Onions
  • 2 Leeks
  • 1 Head of garlic
  • 1 Fresh fennel
  • 1 Bunch celery
  • 1 Bunch fresh thyme
  • 1 Bunch fresh parsley
  • 2 Bay leafs
  • 1 Tablespoon Whole peppercorns
  • 5 pounds bone-in chicken thighs
  • Olive oil

Directions (Read All Before Starting)

Prepare the Ingredients for the Stock

  1. The first (and messiest step) is to prepare the chicken. carefully use a knife to remove the skin and bones from the chicken thighs. You will not need the skins for this recipe, either discard or save for rendering into schmaltz.
  2. Place the chicken bones onto a baking sheet. Drizzle olive oil over the bones and use your hands to make sure they are evenly coated.
  3. Take 3 of the carrots and one of the parsnips and cut off the leaf end. Scrub them to remove any dirt, then gut them into quarters length wise. Place onto a baking sheet.
  4. Take one of the onions, and cut it into quarters WITHOUT removing the skin. The skin will add a nice color and flavor to your soup. Add to the baking sheet with the carrots.
  5. Cut the greens off of both leeks, separate the layers and wash well. Cut the root tip off of both leek whites and discard. Cut one of the leek whites in half, separate the layers and wash. Add the washed white and green layers to the pan with the other vegetables.
  6. Carefully cut the head of garlic in half such that all of the cloves are cut in half. Add the garlic (in its skin) to the vegetable sheet.
  7. Remove the stalks from the fennel and add to the rest of the vegetables.
  8. Break the stalks of celery apart and wash. Put the green center of the celery and all but 3 or 4 stalks onto the vegetable sheet.

Make the Broth

  1. Preheat your oven to 350 degrees.
  2. Drizzle the vegetable baking sheet(s) with olive oil.
  3. Place the chicken and vegetable baking sheets in the oven and roast for 1 hour, flipping every 15 minutes.
  4. Do not allow the vegetables to burn. You want them to be a deep brown, but any burnt vegetable will effect your stock.
  5. Add all vegetables and chicken bones to a stock pot along with: half of the thyme, half of the parsley, the bay leafs, and peppercorns.
  6. Add water to the pot, enough to cover everything by at least an inch.
  7. Bring to a boil over high heat, stirring often to avoid burning.
  8. Lower the temperature to maintain a simmer, cover the pot loosely and simmer for 4 to 5 hours.
  9. If you are making chicken soup, take this time to cook your chicken.
  10. Make sure to stir the pot at regular intervals to avoid burning.
  11. After the broth has taken on a deep color, and your kitchen has become aromatic, carefully use a ladle and a strainer to remove all of the bones and vegetables. Gently press the removed vegetables to extract as much liquid as possible and then discard.
  12. Once all large pieces have been removed and if you wish to use the stock in other recipes as an ingredient, strain the soup into freezer safe containers and store. To continue making soup, strain the stock into a smaller pot.

Finish the Soup

  1. Shred the chicken.
  2. Peel and chop the remaining carrots and parsnip into small, bite sized pieces.
  3. Wash and thinly slice the remaining leek whites and celery stalks.
  4. Remove the root end from the fennel and thinly slice the bulb.
  5. Remove the leafs of the thyme and parsley, and discard the stems.
  6. Add all items to the stock.
  7. Bring to a simmer over medium heat, and simmer until the vegetables are tender.
  8. Serve warm!
