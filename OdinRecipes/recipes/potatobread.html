George Lang's Potato Bread Recipe With Caraway Seeds

A simple, free form loaf of bread that is enriched with mashed potatos.

Ingredients

  • 3 Medium Potatos
  • 1 Package Active Dry Yeast
  • 2 1/2 Cups Warm Water
  • 2 Pounds Unbleached All Purpose Flour
  • 1 1/2 Tablespoons Salt
  • 1/2 Tablespoon Caraway Seeds
  • Cornmeal (Optional)

Directions

  1. Scrub the potatos, and then boil them until tender. When ready, peel and mash them.
  2. While potatos cool, dissolve yeast in 1/2 cup of the warm water.
  3. Mix well with 3 tablespoons of flour.
  4. Let this paste rise for 30 minutes.
  5. Add the remaining two cups of warm water, salt, caraway seeds, flour, and mashed potatos.
  6. Knead the dough 12 to 15 miutes, until it is elastic and full of life.
  7. Shape into a ball and place into an oiled bowl, turing the dough to coat with oil.
  8. Cover and place in a warm spot for 1 to 2 hours, or until doubled in size.
  9. Remove the dough and gently deflate.
  10. Knead for another 4 to 5 minutes.
  11. Shape into a round loaf and place on a buttered 12 inch skillet with rounded sides. Let rise for 30 minutes.
  12. Brush the loaf with water and score a deep X into the top of the loaf.
  13. Bake in a preheated 400 degree oven for 1 hour or until it is nicely browned and sounds hollow when tapped with the knuckles.
  14. Remove bead from pan and allow to fully cool to room temperature before cutting.
